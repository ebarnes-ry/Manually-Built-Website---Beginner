  • top stories of this year
    • The Window Room at Glenstone: To walk through the Glenstone Museum, located in Maryland just 40 minutes drive out of most parts of DC, is a dream of inspiraiton and form. This particular window exhibit reflects what seems to be a recurring motif about the entire campus - we are one with the world.
    • On walking: With most expanses of greenery being somewhat inaccessible to people lacking a car, the Great Falls towpath spanning from Georgetown to Pennsylvania via the Patomac river offers some of the most beautiful sources of sighs no matter the season. Spring is particularly condusive to flower watching, however.
  • this week: featured stories
    • Duster - The Stratosphere Duster was a largely forgotten band from the late 90's that began to grow in popularity in recent years for their ambience. The album references interstellar travel with meaning being derived in their sound, rather than lyrics which - following the shoegaze style - are largely indistinguishable. Many cover bands have popped up, like The BAND playing on 01/29
    • Sculptures at the National Gallery There is a distinctive character in the collections of the national gallery, specifically in their sculptures which you can miander around for hours. Entry is free and hours are pretty regular so I find myself walking the halls and zoning off at the marble for hours.
    • Natural Wine There is something natural about casually meeting old friends at a wine bar and sharing clearly made-to-order food and this place delivers. Off 14th street is a beautiful inlet with plants that moonlight on a saturday catches beautifully. Pop in for drinks and a chat!
